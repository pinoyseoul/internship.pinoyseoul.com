Got Questions?

Frequently Asked

What is the PinoySeoul Internship Program?

The PinoySeoul Digital Media Internship Program (DMIP) is a structured OJT program that gives Filipino college students hands-on experience in broadcasting, digital marketing, business development, and media infrastructure. You work on real projects for a real media company — not simulations.

Is the internship remote or on-site?

The program is hybrid remote. Most work is done online via Google Meet, with occasional on-site events. The schedule is flexible and arranged to meet your school's OJT hour requirements — no daily commute needed.

How long is the internship?

The internship runs for 3 to 6 months (one to two academic semesters), depending on your school's requirements and your progression through the program's mastery-based stages.

What courses or programs are accepted?

We accept students from Multimedia Arts, Broadcasting, Film, Communication, Journalism, Marketing, Advertising, Business Administration, Management, Information Technology, Computer Science, and related programs. Students from other fields may also be considered case-by-case.

What do I get after completing the program?

Upon completion, you receive a Certificate of Completion listing specific skills developed, a LinkedIn recommendation from the Program Director, a professional portfolio of documented work samples, and reference availability for future job applications. Top performers also get access to the Seoul Talents professional network.

Do I need prior experience?

No prior professional experience is needed. You start with self-paced training (Udemy courses) and supervised basic tasks during the first two months. Our mastery-based progression ensures you build competence step by step.

What schedule and time commitment is required?

The schedule is flexible and arranged between you and your supervisor to meet your school's required OJT hours. Sessions happen via Google Meet and are designed to work around daytime classes. You'll participate in nightly collaborative studio sessions where the team works together in real time.
